# Brex

Brex is a corporate financial services platform providing credit cards, expense management, banking, and spend controls for startups and enterprises.

## Overview

**Company**: Brex (San Francisco)
**Founded**: 2017
**Funding**: $1.2B+ (Series D, Tiger Global, Y Combinator)
**Pricing**: Free tier (Essentials), Premium at $12/user/mo, Enterprise custom

## What They Do

Brex provides corporate financial infrastructure:
- **Corporate cards**: High-limit cards for startups without personal guarantees
- **Expense management**: Receipt tracking, policy enforcement, approvals
- **Banking**: Business accounts with yield
- **Bill pay**: Vendor payment automation
- **Travel**: Integrated booking and expense

## Where We Overlap

### P4gent vs. Brex

Both help businesses manage spending. Brex is financial infrastructure; P4gent is spending intelligence.

**Shared concerns**:
- Tracking what you spend
- Managing vendor relationships
- Avoiding unnecessary costs

## Our Differentiation

### Different Value Prop

| Dimension | Brex | P4gent |
|-----------|------|--------|
| **Business model** | Financial services | SaaS subscription |
| **Target** | Startups, enterprise | Individuals, micro-businesses |
| **Core product** | Banking + cards | AI spending assistant |
| **Minimum viable customer** | Company with funding | Anyone with suppliers |
| **Integration** | Financial system | Connects to email/Xero |

### Key Differences

1. **Business stage**: Brex requires a real company. P4gent works for a freelancer day one.

2. **Financial vs. Operational**: Brex is where money moves. P4gent advises on where money should move.

3. **Infrastructure lock-in**: Brex wants all your financial operations. P4gent sits alongside existing tools.

4. **Proactive assistance**: P4gent notices issues and drafts communications. Brex reports what happened.

5. **Relationship context**: P4gent understands supplier relationships. Brex sees transactions.

## Their Advantages

- **Financial infrastructure**: Cards, banking, payments in one
- **Startup ubiquity**: Default choice for VC-backed startups
- **High limits**: No personal guarantee cards are valuable
- **Rewards**: Cash back and points programs
- **Enterprise motion**: Strong upmarket expansion
- **Brand prestige**: "We use Brex" is a status signal
